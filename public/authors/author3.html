Charlotte Brontë

( April 21 , 1816 - March 31 , 1855)

About Charlotte Brontë

Information

Charlotte Brontë, (married name Mrs. Arthur Bell Nicholls, pen name Currer Bell,) an English novelist best known for Jane Eyre (1847), a strong narrative of a woman at odds with her natural desires and social situation. The novel gave Victorian literature a fresh sense of veracity. She went on to write Shirley (1849) and Villette (1849). Patrick Brontë an Anglican minister was her father. He was Irish-born and had changed his name from the more common Brunty. After serving in many parishes, he came to Haworth amid the Yorkshire moors with his wife, Maria Branwell Brontë, and their six little children in the year 1820, having been appointed rector there. Mrs. Brontë and the two eldest daughters (Maria and Elizabeth) died soon after, leaving the father to care for the three surviving girls (Charlotte, Emily, and Anne) and a boy, Branwell. Their upbringing was supported by an aunt, Elizabeth Branwell, who moved from Cornwall to live with the family in Haworth. Charlotte and Emily attended Clergy Daughters' School at Cowan Bridge, near Kirkby Lonsdale, Lancashire, in 1824, together with their elder sisters before their deaths. The fees were minimal, the food was unappealing, and the discipline was tough. Long years later, Charlotte criticized the institution (maybe exaggeratedly) in Jane Eyre, under the disguise of Lowood Institution, and its principal, Reverend William Carus Wilson, has been considered as Mister Brocklehurst's equivalent in the novel. Charlotte and Emily went home in June 1825, and the Brontë children learnt and played there for more than five years, writing and retelling romantic tales for one another and developing creative games to enjoy at home or on the lonesome moors. For over a century, Charlotte's blend of romance and satiric realism had been the norm of practically all female authors. Her fruitful contributions were the telling of a story through the sensibility of a kid or young lady, her lyrical, and the depiction of love from the perspective of a woman.
