My Conclusion & Opinion



Conclusion

In the time Jane Austen lived in, women’s social status was low and they could not achieve economic independence. Though they may feel frustrated and discontented, they were incapable of fighting. However, during Charlotte Brontë's and Louisa May Alcott’s lifetime, the English proletariat was struggling for democratic rights and women's liberation. Women had feminist consciousness and they called for equality between male and female. Even though the time and environment the three writers lived in are different, there are some similarities of character traits between Elizabeth Bennet, Jane Eyre and Jo March. They all advocated that women should have both physical and spiritual independence, not catering men’s taste and severing as an appendage. In little women Jo March is a feminist icon. While her feminism may not be as modern as today's feminism, Louisa May Alcott's Little Women is a serious exploration of women's battle to redefine womanhood and obtain financial independence in a male-dominated world.. But by contrast, Charlotte Brontë’s feminist thoughts are more passionate and assertive. Her yearning for equality is more urgent. Jane Eyre, in comparison to Elizabeth, is more colorful and adventurous she's a plumper flesh-and-blood figure. However, Jane Austen's feminist ideas were further explored by Louisa Mary Alcott and Charlotte Brontë. In order for women to acquire authority during Alcott's time, they had to submit to social standards and make sacrifices that benefited the needs of their husbands and families above their own. Though the influence achieved by each daughter in the Bennet family may appear slight by twentieth century standards, it represents a necessary first step in the march of womanhood. Either way they are all three exceptional female authors, and their works are literary treasures. The way they fought back against society's inequity with amazing bravery still amazes me.
