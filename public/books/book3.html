Jane Eyre

Jane Eyre, the story's orphaned protagonist is an isolated, weak ten-year-old when the tale begins, living with an aunt and cousins who despise her. She becomes more powerful as the tale proceeds. She stands out at Lowood School for her dedication to her studies and intellectual ability. She learns about the pleasures and sorrows of love, as a governess at Thornfield through her relationship with Edward Rochester. When she refuses St. John River's marriage proposal, she returns to Marsh End, where she regains her spiritual focus and realizes her own power. By the end of the story, Jane has grown into a strong, independent woman who is happily married to the man she loves, Rochester.

Edward Fairfax Rochester

Edward is Jane’s lover, he is a brooding, dark figure who had a troubled marriage. Edward married an insane cruel woman named Bertha Mason. Edward sought solace for multiple years in the arms of mistresses. But when he finally seeks to purify his life he wants Jane Eyre, the lovely innocent governess he recruited to educate his foster daughter Adèle Varens, to be his wife. When Jane discovers of his wife's existence, the wedding falls through. When Bertha sets fire to Thornfield, he is punished by losing an eye and a hand as penance for his sins. But when he is reunited with Jane at the end of the tale, he finally finds happiness.

Sarah Reed

Sarah Reed is Jane's obnoxious aunt, who raised Jane until she reaches the age of ten. Despite Jane's best efforts to make amends before her aunt's death, her aunt refuses to budge. She dies without the love of her children and with no remorse for her cruelty of Jane.

Maria Temple

When Jane and Helen’s breakfasts are inedible, the warm-hearted superintendent Maria Temple graciously serves them bread and cheese at Lowood. Miss Temple is a wonderful role model for Jane since she is a brilliant scholar, a model of ladylike etiquette, and a compassionate person. She cares for Helen and Jane, and when Helen is dying Miss Temple provides her a nice, private bed.

Helen Burns

Jane's spiritual and intellectual friend at Lowood is Helen Burns. Helen keeps her elegance despite being wrongly disciplined by Miss Scatcherd at Lowood, thanks in part to her loving friendship with Miss Temple. Jane learns tolerance and serenity from Helen, but she is unable to embrace Helen's rejection of the material world. Jane is motivated to work hard in school because of Helen's impressive intellectual achievements. Helen looks forward to serenity in heaven and eventual reunion with Jane as she dies in Jane's arms.
